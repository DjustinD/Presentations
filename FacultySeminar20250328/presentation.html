1. Introduction and Research Context

Back to Agenda

Introduction

  • Research Question: Do aging limit orders in agricultural futures markets contain less information?
  • Motivation: Desire to understand if some orders contain more information that others

Previous microstructure studies (Cao, Hansch and Wang, 2009; Arzandeh and Frank, 2019) focused on all orders at each price step, but information may not be evenly distributed across all orders.

Historical Context

Traditional Futures Trading

  • Open outcry pit trading
  • Limited data availability
  • Daily price series analyses
  • Focus on end-of-day settlement prices

Modern Electronic Trading

  • Complete order book records
  • Nanosecond timestamp precision
  • Full visibility of limit order lifecycle
  • Ability to track individual order information

Market Microstructure Fundamentals

Key Concepts

  • Limit Order: Price-specific buy/sell instruction
  • Market Order: Immediate execution at best available price
  • Limit Order Book (LOB): Repository of all active limit orders
  • Bid-Ask Spread: Difference between highest buy and lowest sell prices

Market Dynamics

  • Price discovery happens through order interaction
  • Trades occur when market orders meet limit orders
  • Liquidity is provided by limit order volume

2. Background on Limit Order Books

Back to Agenda

Limit Order Book Structure

  • Bids: Buy orders arranged in descending price order
  • Asks: Sell orders arranged in ascending price order
  • Price Steps: Discrete price levels across the order book
  • Market Depth: Volume of orders at each price level

Limit Order Lifecycle

Order Events

  • New: New order placed in the book
  • New orders arrive from a trader
  • Change: Modification to an existing order
  • Orders can have qty changed though a user edit, or a partial trade
  • Order can have price changed through a user edit
  • Delete: Removal of an order
  • Order can be deleted through a user action, or a complete trade

Trade Events

  • Add: New trade occurs

Temporal Characteristics

  • Orders have varying lifespans in the book
  • Maximum lifespan in dataset: 1 week
  • Shorter lifespans for orders that are near market price
  • Longer lifespans for orders that are away from market price

Our research specifically examines how order age affects information content in price discovery.

3. Data and Methodology

Back to Agenda

Market by Orders (MBO) Dataset

  • Time Period: December 2, 2018 to December 27, 2019
  • Markets: Corn and Soybeans futures contracts
  • Data Type: Complete (10 minute snapshots) order book updates
  • Time Precision: Nanosecond timestamps
  • Special Handling:
    • Holidays excluded from analysis
    • Short trading days adjusted accordingly

The MBO dataset provides every single orderbook update, allowing for precise tracking of order age and information content.

Market Activity: Daily Bar Chart

  • Traditional price visualization showing trading ranges
  • Illustrates daily volatility and price trends
  • Provides context for microstructure analysis
  • Shows the market environment during our study period

High-Frequency Market Activity

  • Each tick represents an individual trade
  • Demonstrates market microstructure at fine timescales
  • Shows intraday volatility patterns
  • Reveals market reaction to information arrival

Limit Order Book Visualization

  • Horizontal bars showing bid (buy) and ask (sell) volumes
  • Market depth represented by bar length
  • Price levels on y axis
  • Volume on x axis
  • Bid-ask spread visible at center

Age-Based Order Book Partitioning

  • Orders color-coded by age (new vs. old)
  • New orders (< 30 min) shown in darker colors
  • Old orders ( 30 min) shown in lighter colors
  • Visual representation of our key research concept

Partial Limit Order Books (pLOBs)

  • Definition: Subset of full order book based on specific criteria
  • Age-Based Partitioning Process:
    1. Take a snapshot of all active orders
    2. Calculate the age of each order
    3. Separate orders into "new" and "old" groups
    4. Create partial limit order books for each group
  • Age Thresholds Tested: 10, 20, 30, 40, 50, 60, 70, and 80 minutes

The key insight of our research is that information content varies based on order age.

New vs. Old pLOB Characteristics

New Orders (< threshold minutes)

  • Concentrated near market price
  • Reflect recent information
  • Narrower price distribution

Old Orders ( threshold minutes)

  • Distributed away from market price
  • Lower execution probability
  • May contain stale information
  • Wider bid-ask spread
  • More persistent in the book

Balance between new and old orders varies with the chosen age threshold.

4. Research Models and Information Share Measures

Back to Agenda

Research Objectives

  • Primary Objective: Determine information content of limit orders based on their age
  • Specific Aims:
    • Use order age to truncate or partition the limit order book
    • Measure information share of different order age groups
    • Compare information content across orders of different ages

Model Specifications Overview

  • Variable 1: Last Trade Price
  • Variable 2: New pLOB (Step 1)
  • Variable 3: New pLOB (Steps 2-10)

The model consists of a partial LOB that is built from orders under a certain age cutoff. We will test age limits of 10, 20, 30, 40, 50, 60, 70, 80 minutes, and we will compare with information in an LOB that is built from all orders regardless of age. Further, we will cross-reference our results with the 2019 results from Arzandeh and Frank.

Order Age Groupings

Public Information

  • Persists for < 10 minutes (Lehecka, Wang and Garcia 2014)
  • Quickly reflected in market prices
  • Widely available to all market participants

Private Information

  • Persists for > 10 minutes
  • Slower incorporation into market prices
  • Limited to specific market participants

Experimental Approach:

  • Test age thresholds from 10 to 80 minutes in 10-minute increments
  • Classify orders as "new" if age < threshold
  • Classify orders as "old" if age threshold

Vector Error Correction Model (VECM)

Yt = + 'Yt-1 + l=1p-1lYt-l + ut

Where:

  • Yt = [Pt, WPtnew, WPtold]' (price variables)
  • Pt is the last transaction price
  • WPt are the volume weighted prices for new/old pLOBs
  • is the constant term
  • is the loading matrix
  • is the cointegrating matrix
  • l is a parameter matrix (l=1...p-1)
  • ut is a white noise error term with variance-covariance matrix

Model specifications include 80 lags to cover approximately 10 minutes of trading activity.

Modified Information Share (MIS) Measure

MISj = [F]j2 / [F][F]'

Where:

  • MISj measures variance contribution of price series j to efficient price
  • = FF' with F being a function of innovation correlation matrix
  • represents long-run impact of innovations on prices

This measure allows us to quantify how much of the price discovery process can be attributed to orders of different ages and at different price steps.

5. Results and Analysis

Back to Agenda

Key Findings Overview

  • Information Content: Information persists in orders as old as 60 minutes
  • Information Decay: Order information content declines after 20 minutes
  • Optimal Price Depth: The most valuable information is in the top steps
  • Best Bid/Ask Dominance: Step 1 (best bid/ask) provides 60-70% of all limit order book information
  • Information share of new orders (< threshold) vs. old orders ( threshold)
  • Consistent pattern across all tested age thresholds (10-80 min)
  • Steepest decline in information content occurs around 30-minute mark
  • Old orders retain minimal but measurable information share

Our findings suggest a 30-minute threshold as the critical point where order information begins to significantly deteriorate.

6. Implications for Market Microstructure

Back to Agenda

Theoretical Implications

Information Theory

  • Supports asymmetric information models
  • Confirms information lifecycle in limit orders
  • Quantifies information decay rates
  • Validates public vs. private information persistence patterns

Market Efficiency

  • Information quickly incorporated in nearby price levels
  • Deeper book levels contain less relevant information
  • 30-minute threshold suggests efficient processing speed
  • Consistent with semi-strong form efficiency

Practical Applications

  • For Traders:
    • Focus on newer orders (< 30 min) for price signals
    • Prioritize best bid/ask and nearby price steps for information
    • Develop strategies that account for order information lifecycle

7. Conclusions and Future Research

Back to Agenda

Key Conclusions

  • Order Age Matters: Limit orders lose significant information content after 30 minutes in the book
  • Price Proximity Importance: Best bid/ask and nearby price steps contain most valuable information
  • Robust Findings: Consistent patterns across different market conditions and model specifications
  • Information Efficiency: Agricultural futures markets efficiently process information
  • Market Design: Current market structure shows evidence of effective price discovery

Our research provides empirical evidence that order information decays over time, suggesting market participants should focus on newer orders near the current market price.

Future Research Directions

  • Cross-Market Analysis: Extend methodology to other agricultural and non-agricultural futures
  • High/Low Volume Periods: Compare information dynamics during different trading sessions
  • High/Low Volatility Periods: Does Volatility impact which orders contain inforamtion
  • News Announcement Impact: Study how scheduled news affects information persistence
  • Intraday Patterns: Investigate whether information lifecycle varies throughout the trading day
